*Name and agency have been changed at the founder's request. All financial details are accurate.*

In July 2024, Marcus's digital marketing agency in Sydney posted its best-ever month — $87,000 in revenue, 14 active clients, 6 full-time employees. By January 2025, the agency was in administration.

This is not an uncommon story. But Marcus agreed to tell it in precise detail so that other founders might recognise the patterns before it's too late.

## The Trigger: One Giant Client

At peak, one client represented 61% of the agency's total revenue. A property developer that Marcus had landed two years earlier, who kept expanding the retainer.

"It felt like a gift. In hindsight, it was a time bomb."

When that client's development project stalled due to rate rises, they cut the agency retainer overnight. No notice. The contract allowed it.

Marcus went from $87,000 revenue to $34,000 revenue in a single week. But his cost base — six salaries, two offices, software, insurance — was sized for $80K+ months.

## The Next 30 Days

Marcus did what most founders do in a crisis: he panicked and froze.

- He **didn't restructure fast enough**. He kept all 6 staff for 6 weeks hoping to "win new clients fast."
- He **drew on his personal savings** to cover payroll. $40,000 over two months.
- He didn't tell his accountant or bookkeeper about the situation until month two.

"My accountant told me later that if I'd called her in week one, she could have helped me restructure payroll, access an ATO payment plan for GST owed, and potentially saved two or three jobs."

## What Actually Killed the Agency

It wasn't losing the client. Businesses lose clients. It was a cluster of foundational failures:

1. **No revenue diversification** — 61% from one client is not a business, it's a dependency.
2. **No cash buffer** — the business had less than 3 weeks of cash reserves.
3. **Delayed decisions** — every week of paralysis deepened the hole.
4. **No financial visibility** — Marcus wasn't looking at the P&L monthly. He was checking his bank balance.

## Marcus's Advice

"Have an accountant you speak to monthly, not annually. Know your break-even number. And cap any single client at 30% of revenue — even if they want to give you more. Be disciplined about it."

He's now working for another agency, rebuilding his savings, and planning to start again in 2026. Smaller, slower, more deliberately.

It takes courage to share a story like this. We're grateful Marcus did.
